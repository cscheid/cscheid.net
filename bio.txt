short:

Since 2014, Carlos Scheidegger is assistant professor in the
Department of Computer Science at the University of Arizona. He holds
a PhD in Computing from the University of Utah, where we worked on
software infrastructure for scientific collaboration. His current
research interests are in large-scale data analysis, information
visualization and, more broadly, what happens "when people meet
data". His honors include multiple best paper awards and nominations,
and an IBM student fellowship.
